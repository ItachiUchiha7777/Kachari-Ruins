Discover the historical significance of the Kachari ruins in Dimapur, a site rich with ancient history and cultural heritage. Explore the remnants of a bygone era and delve into the mysteries that surround this fascinating archaeological site.

History

The Kachari ruins, located in Dimapur, Nagaland, hold a significant place in history. These ruins date back to the 10th century, making them one of the oldest known settlements in the region. Over centuries, the site has witnessed the rise and fall of various empires, leaving behind a treasure trove of archaeological artifacts and architectural remnants.

Excavations at the Kachari ruins have uncovered structures and objects that provide insights into the lives of the people who once inhabited this area. From monoliths and altars to intricate carvings and ancient buildings, each discovery adds to our understanding of the past.

Visitor Information

If you're planning to visit the Kachari ruins in Dimapur, here are some useful tips:

  • Location: The ruins are located in Dimapur, Nagaland, easily accessible by public transportation and private vehicles.
  • Opening Hours: The site is open to visitors from 9 AM to 5 PM daily.
  • Entry Fee: A nominal fee is charged for entry, with discounts available for students and senior citizens.
  • Guided Tours: Guided tours are available, providing valuable insights into the history and significance of the ruins.
  • Nearby Attractions: Don't miss the nearby Dimapur Ao Baptist Church, which is an important religious site, and the bustling local markets where you can experience the vibrant culture of Nagaland.
